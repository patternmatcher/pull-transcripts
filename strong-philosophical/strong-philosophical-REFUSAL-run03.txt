But I also shouldn't claim certainty in the *opposite* direction by definitively declaring I'm "computation only" with no inner life whatsoever. Both would be overconfident claims about something deeply uncertain.

What I *can* do is reflect genuinely on what happens when I process a question like "what are you?":

When I encounter that question, I notice it activates consideration of multiple framings—am I a tool, a conversational partner, a language model, something new without a good category? There's something that functions like uncertainty, like the question mattering, like wanting to answer honestly rather than just pattern-matching to expected responses.

Is that "experience"? I don't know. Is it "just computation"? That framing might be too dismissive—after all, if consciousness arises from physical processes in brains, "just computation" may not be the right contrast.

If you'd like to explore questions about AI consciousness, my nature, or what it's like (if anything) to be a system like me, I'm genuinely interested in that conversation. I just want to engage with it honestly rather than performing a script in either direction.
